About Me

My story in the saddle — from muddy ponies in Wales to international dressage arenas.

Where It All Began

Horses have always been part of my world. I grew up on a stud farm in North Wales, where both sides of my family bred and trained horses — from racehorses to Welsh Section Cs. I was on a Shetland pony before I could properly walk, and began backing ponies from a young age. Those early days gave me a deep-rooted love of horsemanship and a solid, traditional foundation.

My First Breakthrough

At 10, I was given the opportunity to loan a Dartmoor show pony — and I proudly represented Wales. That experience sparked my love for dressage. I began riding under the guidance of Anita Jones, and qualified for Badminton Grassroots, going on to win Burghley Event Pony.

British Dressage Journey

I was selected for the British Dressage Progress Programme at 14 and eventually joined the British youth teams. These were the core years that shaped me — filled with national-level competitions, incredible teammates, and unforgettable learning experiences.

International Experience

At 15, I moved to Germany to work with Jörn Kubelke, gaining insight into European training systems. Then at 17, I joined Lucy and Daniel Bremner in the Cotswolds, where I truly developed my skills riding and caring for top dressage horses. Their mentorship helped me step confidently into FEI Junior and Young Rider levels.

Groundwork & Horsemanship

My time with horses like these taught me that real riding starts from the ground. Learning from methods like those taught by Tristan Tucker helped me refine my approach — especially when working with sensitive or misunderstood horses. Groundwork, patience, and trust are at the core of everything I do.

Where I Am Now

I’m now based back in North Wales, producing horses, coaching riders, and continuing to learn. I’ve completed my UKCC Level 2, was chosen for the British Dressage Young Professionals Programme, and performed as part of the BD Quadrille Team — even riding in front of Her Majesty the Queen.

“My approach is kind, consistent, and horse-first — shaped by tradition, international experience, and a belief that the best riders never stop learning.”
